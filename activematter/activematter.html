Why is active matter cool?

As a student of physics, I've been taught many grand and stimulating ideas that describe a huge range of objects that we normally consider 'dead', such as the Hydrogen atom, electromagnetic waves or Bose-Einstein condensates. However, what about the physics that describe the matter that we consider 'alive'? My loose definition of active matter is: a system of particles that are self-propelled. Examples of active matter include flocks of starlings, epithelial tissue in Drosophila, honeybee swarms, bacteria biofilms.

Collective behavior and self-organization, such as flocking or wound healing, emerge when there are sufficient particles in the system. I am curious what the organizing principles of collective behavior in active matter are. I am fascinated by the question of how can one build a scale-invariant theory of active matter that describes everything from the flocking of starlings to wound healing in embryonic epithelia of Drosophila .
