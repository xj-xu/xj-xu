Why is active matter cool?

The last 100 years of physics have been highly successful in building an understanding of matter that is dead - metals, semiconductors, etc. But what about all the matter that we consider alive? Cellular tissue, starling flock, crowds of people running around in a confined space? How can we describe this kind of complex matter that does crazy things. A cohort of cells in the embroyo of a fruit fly can coordinate an impressive feat of closing up a wound without leaving a scar. 104 starlings can perform a dance in the sky without a particular leader. Wouldn't it be cool to really get how they work?
