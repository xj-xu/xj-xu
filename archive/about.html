Brief bio

I was born in the young and modern coastal city of Shenzhen and my family moved to another coastal city Richmond, BC when I was 8.
Growing up, I had lots of time to enjoy skateboarding, snowboarding, playing soccer and hockey. I was a huge Canucks fan... until they lost the cup in game 7 to the Bruins in 2011 (perhaps that shows I'm not a true fan).

Then in 2011, I attended a sixth form college SCIE back in Shenzhen.
At SCIE, I went through an UK curriculum of reading GCSE and A-Levels in physics, mathematics, biology, geography.
I continued to play soccer and captained the team. Near the end of sixth form, I picked up downhill skateboarding - a board sport I had not yet tried. It is quite a thrill and eventually I got good enough to be sponsored by a small local surf board company that also made longboards (Shenzhen Longboard). It was a blast shooting some videos and racing.

In 2015, I moved to Nashville to attend Vandy and started climbing in my sophomore year. I got quite obsessed and it's now pretty much all I do outside of physics during my waking hours. I started a climbing club in my junior year at Vandy and in my senior year, we have started a climbing team competing in the USA Climbing Collegiate Series - super exciting progress!
